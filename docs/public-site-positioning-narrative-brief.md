# Public Site Positioning and Narrative Brief

Purpose:
- reposition the public site from elegant archive toward exhibition portfolio
- define the tone the public site should project
- map the ideal visitor journey across Home, Series, Artwork, and Contact
- identify the current narrative gaps that reduce momentum
- define curation rules for what gets featured publicly

## Positioning statement

Toji Studios should feel like a public-facing exhibition portfolio for digital art and photography, not just a repository of posted works.

The site should communicate that the work is:
- curated, not merely uploaded
- grouped into intentional bodies of work
- presented with atmosphere and context
- available for inquiry, collaboration, licensing, and follow-up viewing

## Target tone

The public site tone should be:
- editorial
- atmospheric
- selective
- confident
- quiet rather than promotional
- curatorial rather than operational

The site should avoid feeling:
- like a stock gallery
- like an asset archive
- like a content feed
- like an artist admin tool exposed to the public

## Core positioning shift

Current state:
- the site is strongest when it behaves like a polished archive with browsing and series structure
- visitors can explore work, but the narrative between sections is still light
- some surfaces still present work as items in a system rather than pieces in an exhibition context

Target state:
- Home frames the practice and sets the curatorial point of view
- Series behaves like exhibition rooms or collections with their own internal pacing
- Artwork pages feel like a focused stop within a larger body of work
- Contact feels like the natural continuation of engagement, not a disconnected utility page

## Ideal user journey

### 1. Home

Job:
- establish what Toji Studios is
- signal the visual and conceptual range of the work
- invite visitors into either curated series or selected individual pieces

What the visitor should understand within the first screen:
- this is a curated exhibition portfolio
- the work has a distinct atmosphere and visual language
- there are structured entry points, not just a large archive

Primary actions from Home:
- enter a featured series
- browse the gallery
- engage with a highlighted piece or featured selection

### 2. Series

Job:
- turn browsing into narrative exploration
- help a visitor understand a body of work as a coherent collection
- surface a few anchor pieces before asking for deeper browsing

What the visitor should feel:
- each series has its own identity and pacing
- the work belongs together for a reason
- the artist is making curatorial choices, not just tagging items with a label

Primary actions from Series:
- open anchor works
- continue through the full series
- move into a related series
- reach an artwork page with stronger context already in mind

### 3. Artwork

Job:
- make the individual piece feel important without severing it from the larger body of work
- preserve context about the series, category, and neighboring work
- offer a clear inquiry/share next step

What the visitor should feel:
- this piece belongs to a larger exhibition context
- there is a reason to continue exploring after viewing it
- inquiry is available when interest becomes intent

Primary actions from Artwork:
- inquire about the piece
- continue to more work from the same series
- move back into gallery or series exploration
- copy/share the piece link

### 4. Contact

Job:
- convert attention into direct conversation
- reassure visitors that inquiries are welcome and relevant
- support commissions, licensing, print requests, and collaboration without sounding generic

What the visitor should feel:
- outreach is expected and easy
- the artist is open to meaningful project conversations
- asking about a specific piece is part of the intended site journey

## Current narrative gaps

### Gap 1 - Home frames the work, but not yet as a full exhibition journey

Current strength:
- the homepage hero language is stronger and more confident than before
- featured, series, and latest sections create multiple entry points

Current gap:
- Home still leans toward browse sections rather than a singular curatorial invitation
- the progression from hero to featured to series to latest can still read as content blocks instead of an authored route through the work

### Gap 2 - Series is stronger than gallery, but still split between overview and immersion

Current strength:
- series pages now provide summary, highlights, and full piece order
- the structure supports exhibition-style browsing better than before

Current gap:
- the overall Series page still begins with list-and-detail browsing mechanics that feel somewhat navigational rather than exhibition-led
- the visitor can still treat series as containers first and narratives second

### Gap 3 - Artwork pages are informative, but still read as object pages more than exhibition stops

Current strength:
- artwork pages show description, metadata, inquiry CTA, and related work
- the same-series follow-through is already present in the “More like this” section

Current gap:
- the page does not foreground the series relationship strongly enough
- the primary read is still image + metadata + CTA, rather than piece within body of work
- there is room to create a stronger handoff back to series context

### Gap 4 - Contact converts well, but the path into it is still mostly functional

Current strength:
- Contact copy is clearer and more conversion-oriented
- inquiry types and response expectations are explicit

Current gap:
- Contact succeeds once reached, but the rest of the site could lead into it more intentionally
- inquiry is still more of a utility action than part of the exhibition narrative

### Gap 5 - Featured content needs stricter curatorial rules

Current strength:
- the homepage already supports featured and latest distinctions

Current gap:
- without a curation standard, “featured” can drift into “recent” or “available” rather than “representative and exhibition-worthy”

## Curation rules for featured public content

Use these rules for homepage featured selections and other public highlight surfaces:

### A featured item should do at least one of these jobs

- represent the strongest visual language of the studio
- anchor a major series
- show range without diluting coherence
- serve as a strong first impression for new visitors
- support inquiries, licensing, or deeper exploration

### A featured set should not be built only from recency

Do not feature work only because it is newly uploaded.

Featured content should be selected by:
- representative quality
- narrative value
- series importance
- visual distinctiveness
- public readiness of title, description, and image quality

### Homepage featured mix guidance

A good featured set should usually include:
- 1 to 2 anchor works that define the current strongest direction
- 1 series-led entry point that encourages deeper browsing
- enough variation to imply range, but not so much that the studio loses identity

### Exclusion rules

Avoid featuring work publicly when it has:
- weak or placeholder titles
- missing descriptions where context is necessary
- low-confidence cover/thumbnail quality
- unclear series placement when it should belong to a body of work
- low editorial confidence from the artist

## Recommended implementation direction

When this brief turns into design and product work, the next public-site decisions should prioritize:

1. Strengthening Home as the curatorial entry point.
2. Making Series pages feel more immersive and less like a two-panel browser.
3. Giving Artwork pages a stronger visible relationship to their parent series.
4. Treating Contact as part of the exhibition journey rather than a detached endpoint.
5. Using stricter featured-content rules so homepage curation feels authored.

## Success criteria

The repositioning is working if a new visitor can quickly understand:
- what kind of work this is
- why the work is grouped the way it is
- where to go next after seeing a single piece
- that inquiry and collaboration are natural outcomes of exploration

## Summary

The public site should present Toji Studios as an exhibition portfolio built around curated bodies of work.

The main strategic move is not adding more pages. It is tightening the narrative continuity between Home, Series, Artwork, and Contact so the visitor feels guided through a point of view rather than left to browse a collection of entries.
