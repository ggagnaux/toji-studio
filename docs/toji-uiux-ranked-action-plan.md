# Toji Studios — Ranked UI/UX Action Plan

## Purpose

This document summarizes the highest-value UI/UX improvements identified from the provided screenshots of the Toji Studios public website and admin application.

The focus is on improving:

- public clarity
- discoverability
- shareability
- conversion potential
- publishing quality
- long-term reach

## Core strategic observation

The site already has a strong visual identity, consistent styling, and a serious admin foundation. The main opportunity is not to make it more decorative, but to make it easier to:

- understand quickly
- navigate confidently
- remember
- share
- act on

In practical terms, the public site should move from feeling like a **minimal private archive** toward a **clear, shareable, search-friendly, conversion-aware exhibition portfolio**.

---

## Tier 1 — Quick wins, high impact

These are the best first moves because they improve public perception quickly without requiring a full redesign.

### 1. Remove `Studio` from the public main navigation
**Why it matters:** It blurs the line between public site and admin interface, and can create unnecessary friction or confusion for visitors.

**Action:**
- Remove `Studio` from the public header
- Keep admin access via direct URL, hidden utility entry, or other non-public method

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Low

### 2. Strengthen the homepage opening message
**Why it matters:** The homepage is elegant, but too quiet. It does not yet communicate identity and purpose strongly enough.

**Action:**
- Rewrite the opening headline and subhead
- Add one clear primary CTA and one secondary CTA
- Make the featured area feel more strategic and intentional

**Suggested structure:**
- headline
- one-sentence positioning statement
- `Explore Gallery`
- `View Series`

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Low to Medium

### 3. Improve public-facing artwork naming
**Why it matters:** Repeated use of “Untitled” weakens memorability, searchability, and shareability.

**Action:**
- Keep internal archival filenames if needed
- Introduce stronger public display names
- Use series-based or study-based naming patterns where appropriate

**Examples:**
- Orb Study 03
- Signal Drift 07
- Field Pattern 02

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 4. Make the Contact page more conversion-oriented
**Why it matters:** Visitors need reassurance, clarity, and a reason to act.

**Action:**
- State what inquiry types are welcome
- Add expected response time
- Clarify services such as licensing, commissions, prints, and collaborations
- Make the call to action more confident and specific

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Low

### 5. Increase text readability site-wide
**Why it matters:** Some body text, labels, and metadata are too faint, too condensed, or too small.

**Action:**
- Raise contrast on secondary text
- Increase body text size slightly
- Improve line height
- Reduce overuse of very faint muted text

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Low

## Tier 2 — Medium effort, major payoff

These changes take more work, but they will make the public site feel stronger and more discoverable.

### 6. Turn series into true landing pages
**Why it matters:** Series are natural units of meaning, memory, and sharing.

**Action:**
- Give each series a stronger intro
- Improve cover image prominence
- Add a short summary for the series
- Feature one to three anchor pieces
- Add related series or an “explore next” path

**Impact:** Very High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 7. Clarify mixed media / content taxonomy
**Why it matters:** Digital art, photography, and other work currently blur together in ways that can weaken positioning.

**Action:**
- Define primary categories more clearly
- Expose medium/type filters more intentionally
- Decide whether the site foregrounds one main identity or multiple distinct content tracks

**Possible categories:**
- Digital Art
- Photography
- Studies / Experiments
- Series

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 8. Reduce boxiness on the public site
**Why it matters:** The public pages sometimes feel too panel-heavy and system-framed.

**Action:**
- Soften or remove some outer containers
- Reduce border density
- Let spacing and rhythm do more of the work
- Allow key sections to breathe more

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 9. Upgrade the artwork detail / lightbox experience
**Why it matters:** This is where interest should convert into deeper engagement.

**Action:**
- Improve title hierarchy
- Clarify series relationships
- Add share / copy-link actions
- Add “inquire about this piece” or similar CTA
- Improve previous/next labeling
- Support a stronger zoom / fullscreen experience

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 10. Improve archive browsing
**Why it matters:** The archive is powerful, but currently feels visually dense.

**Action:**
- Simplify filtering complexity
- Emphasize search and selected filters
- Reduce the feeling of a giant thumbnail wall
- Group or pace results more intentionally where useful

**Impact:** Medium to High  
**Effort:** Medium

---

## Tier 3 — Admin improvements that indirectly grow reach

These changes are less visible publicly, but they improve publishing quality and consistency.

### 11. Add metadata-completeness indicators in admin
**Why it matters:** Public reach improves when every item is packaged better.

**Add indicators for:**
- missing title
- missing description
- missing alt text
- missing tags
- missing series
- missing cover image
- missing social caption

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 12. Improve the upload-to-publish workflow
**Why it matters:** The upload stage is where quality either gets enforced or forgotten.

**Action:**
- Add a publish-readiness checklist
- Prompt for title, description, tags, series, visibility, and thumbnail quality
- Make incomplete public items easy to spot before publishing

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 13. Make Series Manager more editorial
**Why it matters:** Series should be curated, not just stored.

**Action:**
- Highlight missing descriptions
- Preview the public cover image
- Surface public order and visibility clearly
- Add a public page preview link

**Impact:** Medium to High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 14. Prioritize metadata over novelty features
**Why it matters:** Reach comes more from strong titles, descriptions, and publishing systems than from decorative or experimental admin features.

**Action:**
- Treat metadata quality as core product behavior
- Keep splash/banner/AI features secondary to content quality workflows

**Impact:** High  
**Effort:** Mostly prioritization

---

## Tier 4 — Bigger strategic changes

These are the moves that can most meaningfully change long-term reach and distinctiveness.

### 15. Reposition the public site as an exhibition portfolio
**Why it matters:** The site currently leans toward elegant archive. It should feel more like a public-facing exhibition space.

**Action:**
- Strengthen homepage framing
- Improve featured selections
- Clarify page-to-page narrative
- Build stronger transitions between Home, Series, and Artwork views

**Impact:** Very High  
**Effort:** Higher

### 16. Build stronger shareability into public pages
**Why it matters:** Reach grows when pages are easy to send, post, revisit, and remember.

**Action:**
- Ensure unique shareable artwork URLs
- Strengthen series URLs
- Add copy-link actions
- Improve Open Graph previews
- Use stronger titles and descriptions for shared pages

**Impact:** Very High  
**Effort:** Medium to High

### 17. Differentiate public and admin styling more clearly
**Why it matters:** The public site and admin application currently share a lot of UI DNA.

**Action:**
- Public = more atmospheric, spacious, editorial
- Admin = denser, more operational, more information-efficient

**Impact:** Medium to High  
**Effort:** Medium to High

---

## Tier 5 — Validation and infrastructure

These are essential for reach, though some require implementation and testing rather than visual redesign.

### 18. Audit mobile UX thoroughly
**Why it matters:** Reach depends heavily on mobile, and the review materials were desktop-heavy.

**Audit:**
- homepage first impression
- gallery browsing
- series pages
- artwork lightbox
- contact form
- filters
- tap targets
- text size and contrast
- loading behavior

**Impact:** Very High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 19. Audit SEO and social preview metadata
**Why it matters:** Search and sharing are major reach multipliers.

**Ensure:**
- unique page titles
- unique meta descriptions
- Open Graph previews for artwork and series pages
- crawlable public URLs
- enough descriptive public text for indexing

**Impact:** Very High  
**Effort:** Medium

### 20. Audit performance
**Why it matters:** A visually strong but slow site loses traffic and weakens the experience.

**Check:**
- image compression
- responsive image sizing
- lazy loading
- layout stability
- mobile load times
- lightbox smoothness

**Impact:** Very High  
**Effort:** Medium
