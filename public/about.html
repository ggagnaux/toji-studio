About

I make abstract, cosmic-leaning digital artwork—geometry, atmosphere, and controlled chaos. This space is a slow gallery: minimal distractions, maximum presence.

Artist statement

My work lives between structure and drift—hard edges meeting soft fields, like constellations forming and dissolving. I’m interested in how simple systems can produce surprise: repetition, constraint, variation, and the occasional deliberate mistake.

Each piece is built to reward long looks. Up close: texture, micro-choices, and weird little decisions. From across the room: bold shapes and a quiet pull.

If you’d like to license a piece, commission a variation, or talk about a custom backlit print, head to the contact page.

Process

The workflow is part design, part experiment. I start with a constraint (palette, shapes, symmetry, or a rule), iterate until something “clicks,” then refine. Some pieces are fully generative; others are guided by intuition and a lot of ruthless simplification.
